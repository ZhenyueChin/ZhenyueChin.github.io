Paper Notes on U-Net Convolutional Networks for Biomedical Image Segmentation

2019.02.12 Zhenyue Qin Computer Vision

Overview

This paper tackles the problem of limited number of labeled images for training deep networks, where thousands of training images are beyong reach. It can be trained end-to-end from very few images and outperforms the prior best method on the ISBI challenge for segmentation of neuronal structures in electron microsopic stacks.

Methods

U-Net Architecture

The name is due to yielding of a u-shaped structure.

The most prominent property of the u-net is employment the copied feature maps in each pair of symmetric layers, represented in the figure as white boxes. This maximally preserve the extracted feature maps from the contracting path in the symmetric expanding path.

Furthermore, the network weights different pixels differently to reward more for correctly classifying the bounding regions between two objects.

Highlights

  1. Well preservation of extracted feature maps from the contracting path.
  2. Very few training data.
  3. More precise localization.

Improvement

  1. Introduce RoI align, as in Mask R-CNN.
  2. Panelty for discontinuous boundaries of pixels.
